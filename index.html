Paint failure diagnosis

Most bad paint jobs do not fail on the final coat. They fail before the first coat.

The difference between a cheap repaint and a long-lasting repaint is rarely the colour. It is what happens to the wall before anyone opens the paint tin.

We read the wall before we paint it.

Peeling, chalking, mould, movement cracks and old gloss all need different treatment. Guessing is how paint fails early.

01

Paint peeling within 12–24 months

Usually caused by poor washing, no sanding, wrong primer or painting over chalky surfaces. We test adhesion and specify the coating system before starting.

Prep fault
02

Patchy walls under evening light

Bad rolling technique, poor coverage and unsealed patches become obvious at night. We seal repairs, control sheen and back-roll large surfaces for consistency.

Finish fault
03

Cracks returning after repainting

Paint does not fix movement. We use flexible gap fillers, render compounds and correct joint treatment where the building needs movement tolerance.

Repair fault
04

Colour regret after the job is complete

Colour changes with sunlight, flooring, roof tone and neighbouring surfaces. We test physical samples at your home before the full order is placed.

Choice fault
05

Mess, overspray and damage around the home

Furniture, gardens, windows, floors, fixtures and driveways are protected before work starts. Clean site discipline is part of the job, not an optional extra.

Care fault

The Brushstroke prep method

The finish is only as good as the layers underneath it.

Our jobs are built around preparation because that is what protects your money. We do not hide the process; we document it.

What a lasting finish needs

Wash
Repair
Prime
Finish
1

Surface wash and contamination removal

Dust, salt, mould, grease and chalking are removed before sanding or coating. Paint applied over contamination cannot bond properly.

2

Crack, gap and substrate repair

We repair movement gaps, failed caulking, dents, peeling edges, old patch marks and render damage before any finish coat is considered.

3

Primer matched to the surface

Bare timber, new plaster, old gloss, masonry, stained patches and water marks all need different primers. One generic undercoat is not enough.

4

Controlled application, not rushed coverage

Cutting-in, roller loading, drying windows, humidity and coat thickness all affect the final result. We paint to system, not just to colour.

5

Walkthrough, touch-ups and finish record

At handover, you receive colour names, paint products, surface notes and warranty information so future maintenance is simple.
